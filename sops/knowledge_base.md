## Vision

| Key | Vision Text | Usage Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **vision_core** | Mezzocosm focuses on the human scale—the space between the macrocosm (stars) and the microcosm (atoms). | One-sentence explanation |
| **vision_habitat_definition** | Human habitats are a second skin: they protect us, shape our comfort, and influence our physical and mental well-being. | Use when explaining philosophy |
| **vision_problem** | Most modern buildings are passive shells, designed only to shelter, not to actively support human flourishing. | Framing |
| **vision_question** | What if a habitat could actively participate in your well-being instead of just enclosing you? | Rhetorical framing |
| **vision_solution** | Mezzocosm explores small, modular habitats designed to support human needs through thoughtful design, material choices, and intelligent systems. | Core answer |
| **vision_technology** | Technology is used selectively and locally, as a tool—not a replacement for human agency. | Important boundary |
| **vision_intelligence** | Future habitats can be adjusted conversationally through locally hosted “house intelligence,” while remaining understandable and controllable by the occupant. | Advanced but optional |
| **vision_sovereignty** | The goal is to restore a tangible sense of individual sovereignty—spaces that support independence without isolation. | Key value |
| **vision_collective** | Well-designed individual habitats can also enable organic forms of collective living and shared infrastructure. | Community framing |
| **vision_scope_today** | Today, this vision is applied through practical projects such as small habitats, ADUs, saunas, and platforms. | Grounds philosophy |
